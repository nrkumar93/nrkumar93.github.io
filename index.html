 Ramkumar Natarajan

I am currently a Visiting Scholar at the Search Based Planning Lab in Robotics Institute, Carnegie Mellon University and also a Robotics Researcher at RobotWits LLC where I am working with Prof. Maxim Likhachev. I obtained my Master's of Science in Robotics Engineering at Worcester Polytechnic Institute (WPI) in 2017 where I was advised by Prof. Michael Gennert for my thesis. While doing my Master's, I worked in parallel at Bossanova Robotics in the perception and autonomy systems of a retail store robot. Previously, I worked at the Reconfigurable and Intelligent Systems Engineering (RISE) lab for my Bachelor's thesis under the supervision of Prof. Balaraman Ravindran at Indian Institute of Technology (IIT), Madras. I graduated from Sastra University, Tanjore with a Bachelor's degree in Electrical and Communications Engineering in 2015. During this time, I worked with Prof. Manigandan and Prof. Ramkumar on mobile robot localization and mapping.

 

Research Interests

My research interests include: Motion Planning, Control Theory, Machine and Reinforcement Learning, Localization & Mapping and Formal methods with applications to Robotic systems. In my research, I like to draw inspiration from abstract fields of Mathematics like Algebraic Graph Theory, Differential Equations and Topology.
